What Was the Halifax Gibbet?"

The Halifax Gibbet was a wooden execution device used from the 13th to 17th centuries.

It consisted of a tall wooden structure with a heavy axe blade mounted on a sliding block, designed to swiftly behead criminals.

It predated the French guillotine by over 200 years and was central to law enforcement in Halifax, especially to protect the townâ€™s cloth industry.

The Gibbet was used primarily for punishing theft, particularly of cloth, and symbolized the town's harsh justice system.
